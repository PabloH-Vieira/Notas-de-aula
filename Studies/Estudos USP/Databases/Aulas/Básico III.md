There are two words for “where”.

- The standard version is “_wo_”

- If it implies _where to_ it is “_wohin_”

  

The preposition to can be extracted from “_where to_” being simply “_hin”._ For this reason, “to go to” is “_hingehegen_”

  

There are also two versions for _there_

- there - dort

- to there - dorthin

  

> When do you want to go there? - Wann wollen Sie dorthin gehen?

> I want to go there, but I cannot go there today - Ich will dorthin gehen, aber Ich kann nicht heute dorthin gehen

> I am - Ich bin

  

busy - beschäftigt (”beschéf-tirrt”

business / store - das Geschäft (”_guechéft_”)

  

> I am very busy - Ich bin sehr beschäftigt

> I will be very busy - Ich werde sehr beschäftigt sein

> I am very busy now - Ich bin sehr beschäftigt jetzt

The imperative form is written as same as the interrogative. The only difference is on pronunciation, so it must be a point of attention.

  

towards here - her

To wait - warten

to wait for - warten auf

> Will you please wait for me - Wollen Sie auf mich bitte warten

> Are you waiting for me? - Warten Sie auf mich?

> Will you please wait for me? - Wollen Sie auf mich warten, bitte?

> Where will you wait for me? - Wo werden Sie auf mich warten?

Sie also means “they”, so it can be a little bit confusing.

If it is not clear wheter “you” or “they” is meant, then we use “die” (_di_)

  

to buy - kaufen

> We will not buy it - Wir werden es nicht kaufen

  

expensive - teuer (”_toier_”)

> We are going to buy it because we want to have it - Wir werden es kaufen denn wir wollen es haben

  

There is a second way to express future tense, which is not present in English. It is done by using present tense.

> I see you tomorrow - I sehe Sie morgen

  

He - er

_Werden_ (will) is conjungated as “_wird_” (”_virrt_”) _to he, she, it._

> He will - er wird

  

She - sie (non capital)

- Sie with capital S is used both for you and they whereas _sie_ with a non capital S means she.

> She will be here soon - Sie wird hier bald sein

Usually, verbs when following the subjects he/she/it comes with a “t”

  

> He stays - Er bleibt

  

When refering to a group of people (all of them, everybody) we use _alle._

  

my friend - Main Freund

my friends - Maine Freunde

  

> We are all staying here - Wir bleiblen alle hier

> He is coming soon - Er kommt bald

> He is bringing it to me - Er bringt es mir

> He will bring it to you tomorrow - Er wird es ihnen morgen bringen

> When will you bring it to me? - Wann werden Sie es mir bringen?
