> What → was (pronounced “vass”)

> Water → wasser (pronounced “vass”)

  

eu is pronounced “ói”

  

The infinitive form of verbs ends with “n” or “en”.

- To Drink → trinken

- To eat → essen

- to do → tun

- to come → kommen

  

Whenever a sentence has two verbs, the second one always come at the end of the sentence.

> Do you want to come with me? → Wallen Sue mit mir kommen?

  

The word _heute_ can be also used to reference a period in that day, such as day, evening or morning.

> Do you want to come with me this evening? → _Wallen Sie mit mir heute abend kommen?_ or _Wallen Sie heute abend mit mir kommen?_

  

_Wallen Sie_ is both used for “do you want to” and “will you please to”
